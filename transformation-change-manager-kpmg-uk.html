The KPMG Delivery Network (KDN) is a KPMG special purpose member firm offering a way for clients to leverage KPMG top talent and technology platforms through regional teams of specialists, enabling economies of scale and a new way of working that expands beyond local capability Together with KDN, KPMG member firms can drive the sales and delivery of global solutions at a competitive price and in a repeatable and consistent manner. As a member of KDN, you’ll be a part of the KPMG family working alongside some of our profession’s most skilled practitioners on rewarding programs and initiatives that are changing the way business operates, delivering value to our clients, and driving positive change in the communities we serve. You’ll be enabling KDN accelerate new ways of working, using cutting-edge technology and working together with our member firms located in nearly 150 countries to help us achieve our ambition to be the most trusted and trustworthy professional services firm. And through your work, you’ll build a global network and unlock opportunities that you may not have thought possible with access to great support, vast resources, and an inclusive, supportive environment to help you reach your full potential. Role Summary As the Transformation Communications Manager, you will support the Transformation Communications and Change Lead with planning and execution of Transformation communications. This is a challenging role that will involve day-to-day liaison with KDN Leaders. All outputs and outcomes must be to the highest standard and the successful candidate will need to display exceptional communication and influencing skills, as well as a keen sense of business acumen and professional judgment. Partnering with KDN team members to identify creative and effective ways to communicate with various KPMG audiences. This role involves the creation and delivery of communication plans, stakeholder maps and a seamless integration with project teams and change management In your role, you will help shape communications to drive change adoption and efficiency, minimise disruptions and optimise outcomes during the transition period. You will provide SME guidance to project managers and teams while managing stakeholder relationships and change throughout the transition. global team (early morning and/or late evening meetings, ensuring those you’re working with in different time zones have what they need to continue work during their day, etc.)• Collaborate with cross-functional teams, including HR, IT, Finance, Operations, and Legal and the Global Delivery Centres Transformation Communications:• Manage multiple projects or programs, either as a sole contributor, or as part of a team of professionals• Operationalise knowledge of transformation communications best practices, employing them consistently• Work with the business and project teams to implement communications strategies to support colleagues and stakeholders during transitions• Chart communications delivery timelines, milestones, and deliverables to track progress and ensure timely completion• Coordinate closely with relevant teams to ensure alignment of communications and to minimize disruptions to ongoing operations Stakeholder Engagement:• Communicate and manage expectations
